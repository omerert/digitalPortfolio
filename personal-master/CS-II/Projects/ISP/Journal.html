Roles

(software) Development Engineer

A software development engineer is responsible for designing, testing, and maintaining software systems that meet the needs of users. They work on various aspects of the software development process, including coding, debugging, and quality assurance. They may also collaborate with other team members, such as project managers, designers, and business analysts, to ensure that the software being developed is effective, efficient, and meets the goals of the project. Ultimately, the primary goal of a software development engineer is to create high-quality software that meets the needs of users and helps organizations achieve their objectives. I might not be the best fit for this role since my understanding of Swift is not the highest.

QA Engineer

A QA, also known as quality assurance, engineer is responsible for ensuring that the software being developed meets the necessary standards of quality: designing and implementing testing procedures, executing tests, and identifying and tracking defects. QA engineers work closely with software development engineers to identify and resolve defects and improve the overall quality of the software. They may also be responsible for creating and maintaining documentation related to the testing process, such as test plans and bug reports. I might be a good fit for this role since, in my own projects, I am able to identify how I can make my project better by running realistic test.

Project Manager

A project manager is responsible for: planning, organizing, and directing the work of a team to achieve a specific goal. This includes establishing project goals and objectives, creating project plans, and defining roles and responsibilities for team members. Project managers also play a key role in budgeting and resource allocation, and may be responsible for tracking and reporting on project progress to stakeholders. They may work with a variety of team members, including developers, designers, and business analysts, to ensure that the project stays on track and is completed within budget and on schedule. I feel comfortable in being in this role since I feel confident in managing the process of the ISP.

Product Manager

A product manager is responsible for defining and delivering a product that meets the needs of the market and the business. This involves conducting market research to identify customer needs and working with cross-functional teams, such as engineering, design, and sales, to bring the product to market. Product managers may also be responsible for setting the product vision and strategy, defining the product roadmap and feature set, and managing the product lifecycle. They may work closely with stakeholders, such as customers, sales teams, and executives, to ensure that the product meets the needs of the market and aligns with the overall business strategy. I feel that I can be a good fit for this role since I am good with researching and communication with those associated with the ISP.

Release Engineer

A release engineer is responsible for the development and management of the processes and tools used to build, test, and deploy software releases. This may involve automating the build, test, and deployment process, setting up and maintaining continuous integration and delivery systems, and working with development teams to ensure that releases are stable and reliable. Release engineers may also be responsible for managing code branching and merging, creating and maintaining documentation related to the release process, and working with other teams to coordinate the release of new features and updates. I believe that I can be a good fit for this role since I can make updated version of our ISP to make it better.
